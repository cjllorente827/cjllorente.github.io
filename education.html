Other Research Work

Research Experience for Undergraduates 2018

Advised by Dr. Lisa Kaltenegger
Over the summer at Cornell University, I worked on an atmospheric simulation code called ATMOS to run simulations of Earth-like planets. My goal was to create mock observations of light passing through the atmospheres of these planets in order to inform analysis of real exoplanets in the hope of finding terrestrial, habitable exoplanets. My experiments ultimately showed that increasing surface pressure led to an upwards scaling of atmospheric features, such as the ozone layer. These features would form at much higher altitudes on such a planet, potentially making observations of these planets easier to detect. I presented the results of this work at the 233rd meeting of the American Astronomical Society and won the Chambliss Student Award for my poster.
